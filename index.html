Engineering perspective

Useful technology starts with a clear problem and ends with evidence.

My work sits across electronics, firmware, connectivity, edge computing, and user-facing software. I’m interested in systems that operate in the real world—under physical, timing, power, and reliability constraints.

I approach projects as complete engineering stories: define the operating context, choose the right sensors and compute platform, design the data path, validate behavior, and communicate the result clearly. As an educator, I also care about making difficult technical ideas understandable and reproducible.
